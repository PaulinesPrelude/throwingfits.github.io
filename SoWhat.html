SO WHAT?

Inequality

SCIENCE. FASHION. OUTDOORS.

Aside from exploration, these fields have an unfortunate similarity: the prevalence of gatekeeping from BIPOC communities.

Like many other disciplines and pasttimes, these have historically been dominated by White people, preventing so many opportunities for exploration and contributions to the fields.

What can we do?

As (possibly) outdoor, fashion, or science fanatics, there are many things we can do to be inclusive, and here are just a couple of ideas:

  • Donate to organizations that make the outdoors more accessible
  • Lend or donate gear
  • Spread the word!

Good news

Thanks to the internet and our ever evolving digital age, the way these fields look is changing.

Now more than ever, online presense is given more weight in these industries. Luckily, it makes it easier for a diverse group of people to have opportunities, given how accessible the internet is.

Virgil Abloh is just one example of a person gaining success in our digital age. As the first Black artistic director at Louis Vuitton, he serves as an inspiration for many internet people today.
